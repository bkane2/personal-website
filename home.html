Bio

I'm a first-year PhD student of Computer Science at the University of Rochester. My current research interests and experience lie within knowledge representation & commonsense reasoning, natural language processing, and dialogue planning. With a background in Economics, I am also equally interested in practical applications of these technologies toward real-world domains.

Under the advisorship of Dr. Lenhart Schubert, some projects that I have worked on include:

  • Designing an dialogue manager for a virtual agent capable of holding a series of open-ended conversations with older adults, with the aim of providing dynamic feedback on conversational skills.

  • Applying machine learning models to predict turn-taking in open-ended human-computer dialogue, using various features relating to syntactic and semantic completion as well as speech prosody.

  • Annotating a varied corpus of natural-language sentences with unscoped logical form (ULF) representations, and creating code to generate Natural Logic-like inferences over ULF for a set of clause-taking verbs.

  • Working on code to generalize concepts through feature embeddings, for use in inferring rich schematic knowledge by matching certain primitive schemas ("protoschemas") to simple children's stories.
